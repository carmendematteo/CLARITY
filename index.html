Return on Invested Capital (ROIC)

?
Measures how efficiently a company converts capital into profit. >20% = elite moat. 10–20% = good. <8% = weak. Negative = destroying value.

What it does: Measures how efficiently a company turns invested capital into profit. Values over 20% indicate a massive competitive moat.

ROIC = [Operating Profit × (1 − Tax Rate)] ÷ (Total Debt + Equity − Cash)

Margin of Safety · 25%

Margin of Safety

?
Buffer against being wrong. >20% = solid. >40% = deep value (check for traps). Negative = overvalued.

What it does: Provides a buffer against being wrong. Enter total company value + shares outstanding to auto-calculate per-share intrinsic value, or enter per-share values directly.

Margin of Safety = (Intrinsic Value − Current Price) ÷ Intrinsic Value
Probabilistic · 10%

Expected Value

?
Weighs potential gains vs losses mathematically. Positive EV = favorable bet. Loss probability is auto-calculated as 1 − Win Probability.

What it does: Weighs potential gains against potential losses. Helps you decide if the risk is worth the reward mathematically.

Expected Value = (Win Probability × Profit) − (Loss Probability × Loss)
Position Sizing · 10%

Kelly Criterion (Position Sizing)

?
Half-Kelly prevents over-leveraging. Suggests a conservative % of your portfolio to allocate. Higher reward:risk ratio = larger suggested allocation.

What it does: Prevents over-leveraging. We use a "Half-Kelly" strategy to suggest a conservative position size for your portfolio.

Half-Kelly = [Win Probability − (Loss Probability ÷ Reward:Risk Ratio)] × 0.5

Valuation · 35%

MVRV Ratio

?
Market Value to Realized Value. <1.0 = holders underwater (historical bottom zone). >3.0 = historically overheated. 1.0–3.0 = neutral range.

What it does: Measures the "Pain Index". Compares Market Price to what everyone actually paid. Under 1.0 means holders are at a loss — historically a bottom signal.

MVRV = Market Cap ÷ Realized Cap
Usage · 30%

NVT Ratio

?
Network Value to Transactions. Like a P/E ratio for crypto. <30 = strong on-chain usage. 30–100 = normal. >100 = overvalued relative to usage.

What it does: The "P/E Ratio" of crypto. Compares the network's value to the actual money being moved across it.

NVT = Market Cap ÷ Daily On-Chain Transaction Volume
Network Growth · 20%

Metcalfe's Law Score

?
Values the network based on the square of its users. Lower score = price growing in line with (or below) network growth. Higher = price outpacing users (overheated).

What it does: Values the network based on the square of its users. Identifies if a coin's price is growing faster than its actual user base.

Score = Market Cap ÷ (Active Addresses²)
Market Psychology · 15%

SOPR — Spent Output Profit Ratio

?
SOPR <1.0 = sellers realizing losses (capitulation, often a local bottom). >1.0 = profit taking. Sustained >1.0 = bull market.

What it does: Are people selling at a profit or loss? Values under 1.0 indicate capitulation — often a signal of a local bottom.

SOPR = Value of Coins When Sold ÷ Value of Those Same Coins When Bought
